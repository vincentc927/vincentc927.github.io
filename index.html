Top Things to Do in South Philly
Melrose Diner

Let the neon lights welcome you to Melrose Diner, open 24/7. This iconic eatery has an Art Deco exterior and warm lighting that falls on the leather booths in the interior, the way diners should be. It’s a quiet, unassuming place to get a cheesesteak or comfort food. Pro-tip: the desserts are pretty good, and we highly recommend the butter layer cake.

Passyunk Restaurant Row

East Passyunk Avenue has one of the top 10 “Restaurant Row”s in the nation. People from all over the country visit Le Virtù (fine Italian) and Stateside (everything is made in the U.S.). On Passyunk & Morris, Barcelona (wine bar), Cantina Los Caballitos, and Bing Bing Dim Sum are hopping with activity on most nights – especially the weekend. Pro-tip: For a really cool, low-key place, Townsend has excellent food and drinks. Also, POPE is a well-known bar that unites gritties and hipsters alike.

Geno's & Pats Steaks

These two iconic, outdoor rival cheesesteak spots are world famous. At Geno's, check out the endless photos of presidents and movie stars who’ve patroned there. At Pat’s, observe the piece of sidewalk in which Sylvester Stallone stood during the filming of Rocky. The eternal question – or debate – for Philadelphians is Geno’s or Pat’s: which is better? Try both and see for yourself!
